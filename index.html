Education

University of California, Berkeley

B.S. in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science • December 2018
Minor: Linguistics

I'm currently finishing my first year at UC Berkeley, where I've been involved with undergraduate research in both Computer Science and Nuclear Engineering, as well as teaching in the Electrical Engineering Department. Additionally, I'm working on the project management team for Berkeley's Engineers Without Borders chapter, where we're working to bring a centralized water distribution system to a small village in Panama.

Work

UC Berkeley Electrical Engineering Division

Academic Intern • January 2016 - Present

As part of the course staff for Berkeley's introductory Electrical Engineering class, I instructed students in fundamental laboratory skills such as circuit design, soldering, and debugging with oscilloscopes as they were being exposed to such techniques for the first time. Additionally, I assisted students in the use of Python with NumPy for scientific computing, especially in the area of signal processing.

Research

UC Berkeley Department of Nuclear Engineering

Radiation Detection • February 2016 - Present

As a member of the DoseNet on UC Berkeley's and LBNL's RadWatch project, I'm working on both the hardware and software side of Raspberry Pi based dosimeters. These devices are installed in Bay Area high schools, where they help students learn about radiation and detection. (They also give us valuable data about Bay Area background radiation levels)

UC Berkeley Computer Sciences Division

Computational Game Theory • January 2016 - Present

Member of the Gamescrafters Computational Game Theory research group. Using Python with NumPy and MPI, we're building a distributed, generalized solver for two-player abstract strategy games to be deployed to Berkeley's Savio supercomputing cluster. Our current goals are to be the first researchers to strongly solve Reversi, and to make our engine open and easily usable by the public. See my contributions here.
